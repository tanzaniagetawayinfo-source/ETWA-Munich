What we do

Charitable funding for self-help projects worldwide

We generally support small-scale projects of genuine immediate benefit to those involved, with a strong preference for initiatives that come directly from the people concerned.

We provide charitable funding of self-help projects worldwide, focusing on small-scale initiatives that create immediate and tangible impact for the communities involved.

Our work spans diverse areas, from women's self-empowerment in India, to education for street children in Guatemala, to supporting vocational infrastructure such as carpentry workshops in Ghana. Each project is practical, local, and designed to solve real problems.

What makes ETWA different is how projects originate: they come directly from the people concerned, usually through trusted personal connections within our network. This ensures that funds reach the right people and are used effectively.

Because all organisational work is carried out voluntarily, we operate with virtually no administrative overhead. This means that almost every contribution goes directly to supporting projects on the ground.

How it works

A transparent, member-driven process

Every project follows a clear and accountable path — from proposal to funding to reporting.

1. Project proposed

Initiatives come directly from trusted local contacts, ensuring real needs and real impact.

2. Members review

Projects are discussed collectively during members’ meetings, with full transparency.

3. Funding approved

Only projects approved by members receive funding, ensuring shared responsibility.

4. Results reported

Progress and outcomes are documented and shared through reports and updates.

Who does what

A volunteer-led organisation

The association is organised and managed by volunteers who coordinate projects, meetings, finances and reporting.

Committee members

Elected by members at the Annual General Meeting, they handle organisation, pre-evaluation of new project proposals, meetings, minutes, fund administration and membership support.

Treasurer & auditor

The treasurer maintains the accounts, while an auditor appointed at the Annual General Meeting reviews the financial reports.

Chairperson

Responsible for overall coordination and for writing the annual reports.

Local contact persons

A wider network of volunteers supports the association through trusted local and project-related contacts.
