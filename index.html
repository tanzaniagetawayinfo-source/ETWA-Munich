Keeping you informed

ETWA combines member participation with ongoing transparency so supporters can follow decisions, reports, and the progress of funded work.

01

Members' meetings

Meetings give members the opportunity to hear about projects, discuss priorities, and take part in decisions on which initiatives should receive funding.

02

Project approval

Projects must be approved by members before funding can be provided, ensuring a participatory and accountable process.

03

Online documentation

ETWA documents, latest news, annual reports, financial reports, and auditor's reports are made accessible through the ETWA homepage and document library.

Where the money comes from

The Association is supported through member contributions, donations, and fundraising activities that together sustain its charitable work.

Memberships

The most important source of funding comes from members and the contributions they make. Membership is open to anyone. Members contribute around one thousandth of their annual salary each year, and many choose to give considerably more.

Donations

Additional support comes from private donations, collecting boxes, and gifts in kind such as books, CDs, and records.

Sales and fundraising

Events and fundraising initiatives such as fairs, tombolas, books, and greeting cards help expand ETWA's impact.

Efficient allocation

Because administrative work is done voluntarily, funds can remain focused on projects rather than being absorbed by internal overhead.

Who does what

ETWA's structure is built on volunteer commitment, elected responsibilities, financial oversight, and local contacts who help maintain trusted relationships.

A

Committee

The committee pre-evaluates new project proposals, arranges meetings, writes minutes, administers funds, and encourages membership. Members are elected at the Annual General Meeting.

B

Treasurer & auditor

Accounts are maintained by a treasurer, and financial reports are audited by an auditor appointed at the Annual General Meeting.

C

Chairperson & volunteers

The chairperson coordinates the overall work and writes annual reports, while a network of volunteers acts as local contact persons.
